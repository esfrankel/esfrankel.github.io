About


Me with a rather unfortunate haircut. I’m Eric Frankel, a mathematician, amateur runner, and bad haircut enthusiast in the Bay Area.

I study math and a mixture of computer science and economics at Stanford. Currently, I'm particularly interested in analysis and probability theory; game theory, market design, and incentives; and machine learning theory and its applications. My resume and LinkedIn reflect my pursuit of these interests through a variety of experiences.

I fundamentally believe that there's a story in every dataset, and every story has a dataset in it. It's my goal to find that story in a variety of disciplines and landscapes. I hope that some of my writings in my blog might be effective at telling that story.

I did research with Michael Bernstein’s group in the Stanford AI Lab and the Stanford HCI Lab on generative models. In the past, I’ve worked at Lumi Labs and the Translational Genomics Research Institute.

When I'm not focused on my academic interests, I like working on side projects with my friends, running slower than I would like, visiting exciting places, and eating delicious food.
